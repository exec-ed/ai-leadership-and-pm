Six Operational Frameworks

Proven frameworks to guide decision-making throughout the AI project lifecycle.

1

Stakeholder Management

Power-Interest Matrix for identifying and managing stakeholder influence and engagement throughout your AI project.

Use Case: Map project stakeholders, identify champions and resistors, plan engagement strategies.
2

Pilot Design

The Goldilocks Principle for scoping pilots that are "just right" - not too small to be meaningless, not too large to be risky.

Use Case: Define pilot scope, select appropriate test locations, balance learning vs. risk.
3

Crisis Response

Four-step protocol for handling AI project crises: Diagnose ‚Üí Decide ‚Üí Communicate ‚Üí Document.

Use Case: Technical failures, ethical issues, stakeholder conflicts, or unexpected outcomes.
4

Ethical Decision-Making

Four critical questions to evaluate ethical implications: Who benefits? What could go wrong? How do we know? When do we stop?

Use Case: Bias concerns, privacy issues, fairness questions, or stakeholder value conflicts.
5

Scale/Pivot/Kill Criteria

Decision framework for determining whether to scale successful pilots, pivot struggling initiatives, or kill failing projects.

Use Case: Post-pilot evaluation, quarterly project reviews, investment continuation decisions.
6

Change Management

Integrated change management strategies woven throughout the project lifecycle to ensure adoption and sustained impact.

Use Case: User adoption, organizational resistance, process changes, cultural transformation.

Hands-On Exercises

Four immersive exercises using the RetailFlow case study to practice real-world project management challenges.

Exercise 1: Stakeholder Speed Dating

Role-playing activity

45 min

Practice stakeholder engagement by role-playing conversations with six different RetailFlow stakeholders, each with unique concerns about the AI chatbot project.

Key Learning: Adapt your communication style and message to different stakeholder needs and concerns.

Exercise 2: Pilot Goldilocks

Case analysis

45 min

Evaluate three pilot scope options for RetailFlow's chatbot deployment. Determine which scope is "too small," "too large," or "just right" using the Goldilocks framework.

Key Learning: Balance learning objectives, risk tolerance, and business impact when scoping pilots.

Exercise 3: Crisis Simulation

Live simulation - THE CORE ACTIVITY

90 min

The heart of the masterclass. Experience four cascading crises in real-time: technical failure, ethical dilemma, stakeholder conflict, and competitive pressure. Make decisions under time pressure with incomplete information.

Key Learning: Apply all frameworks under realistic crisis conditions. Practice rapid decision-making, stakeholder communication, and ethical reasoning.

Exercise 4: Scale or Kill Decision

Investment review

30 min

After the crisis simulation, evaluate whether to scale the RetailFlow chatbot to all 50 stores, pivot the approach, or kill the project using systematic decision criteria.

Key Learning: Make honest post-pilot assessments. Don't fall for sunk cost fallacy. Scale successes, pivot underperformers, kill failures.
